Identifying Business Tasks¶

Cyclistic, a bike-sharing company handles two type of customers: casual customers who bought single-ride or daily passes, and Cyclistic members who bought the annual pass. The stakeholder believes that maximizing the number of Cyclistic member is the key of the company's growth as annual pass are deemed more profitable. The first step of the marketing strategy targetted toward the casual customer is to understand how the casual and member riders differ, especially in how they use the service.

As a data analyst, my tasks would be: analyse the difference of riding habit between casual and member riders and create a recommendation for the stakeholder based on it. This habit may include: the duration and distance of a ride and type of service used.

Preparing Data for Analysis¶

In this step, I will be giving some background information of the data that will be used, load the data for analysis and preparing it for further transformation and cleaning in the next step of analysis.

I will be using Divvy's public data that is made available by Lyft Bikes and Scooters, LLC ("Bikeshare") and City of Chicago under this license and can be accessed here.

How reliable is this data?¶

The data we will be using is gathered directly by City of Chicago's Divvy bicycle sharing service and published freely as part of their commitment to supporting bicycling as an alternative transportation option. In my case, Cyclistic is an imaginary company so for this case study, we will be treating Divvy's data as Cyclistic's own data. Thus, we can consider the data we will be using is a first-party data which is the most reliable kind of data.

Column Description¶

Here are some short description of the data based on my observation:

  • ride_id is the identifier for each ride's record.
  • rideable_type is the type of ride that the customer choose for each ride.
  • started_at is when the ride started.
  • ended_at is when the ride ended.
  • start_station_id is the id of the station where the ride started.
  • end_station_id is the id of the station where the ride started.
  • start_lat is the latitude of the location where the ride started.
  • start_lng is the longitude of the location where the ride started.
  • end_lat is the latitude of the location where the ride ended.
  • end_lng is the longitude of the location where the ride ended.
  • member_casual is the status of the user (member or casual).

Conclusion and Recommendation¶

Based on our analysis, we can conclude that Casual riders tend to spend more time on a ride compared to members. This could happen because they mostly use bike sharing for "recreational" purposes which can be supported by how their usage peaked in weekend. In comparison to members who opted for the annual plan due to an habitual need and mostly have a "purposeful" trip.

Based on those findings, Our recommendation to maximize members are:

  • Offer a free try-out period for casual riders to use the service in weekday. This will encourage casual riders to try using the bikesharing even for their "purposeful" trip and if they find this option is useful, they can subscribe to the annual plan and become a member.
  • Offer a weekend-only annual plan which will attract a casual riders who can not use the service in weekday. This may be beneficial for those who has to commute to work but prefer using bike for working out or as a mode of transportation in weekend.
